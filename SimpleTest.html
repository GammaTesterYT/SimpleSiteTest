1. Types of numbers

  • Natural numbers are all  numbers from one to infinity. (1, 2, 3)
  • Whole numbers are all numbers from zero to infinity (0, 1, 2, 3)
  • Integers are whole numbers and their negatives. (-3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3)
  • Rational numbers are any number that can be the ratio between two integers. (1:3.14)
  • Irrational numbers are numbers that cannot be the ratio between two integers because they have infinite decimals without repeated patterns. (3.1415926535)
  • Real numbers are all irrational and rational numbers or numbers that can be used to measure a continuous one-dimensional quantity (length, temperature, duration)
  • Complex numbers are numbers that has real numbers and the imaginary unit ‘i’(1+3i)

1.2. Fractions

Fractions show how many parts you have out of the total number.  A fraction has 2 parts, the top number is called a numerator and the bottom number is called a denominator, the denominator is generally greater than the numerator.  In 2/4, you have 2 parts out of 4, or half.

Improper fractions are fractions with the numerator being greater than the denominator, meaning it represents the whole number or more. They can be converted into mixed numbers by dividing the numerator by the denominator.

                

Addiction

To make the addiction of fractions, add the numerators and if the denominators are the same, don’t change its value. If the denominators are unlike, multiply them by each other then multiply the first numerator by the second denominator and the second numerator by the first denominator and add them up. the numbers together means they’re being multiplied by each other. (ad = a x d)

Subtraction

To make the subtraction of fractions, multiply the denominators by each other then multiply the first numerator by the second denominator and the second one by the first denominator, then subtract each other.

Multiplication

To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators and denominators together to get the result.

Division

To divide fractions, keep the first one, change division to multiplication and then flip the second fraction turning it on a reciprocal to get the result.

Improper fractions into mixed numbers

To turn improper fractions into mixed numbers, divide the numerator by the denominator. The result will be the mixed number which is the quotient and the remainder will be the new numerator.

1.3. Place values

Place value is the value of each digit in a number that changes it is determined by its position. The most left number is the one with the greatest value while the most right number is the one with the smallest value. There are 9 numbers that represent each value. Numbers can be infinite.

1.4. Decimals

Decimal places are for magnitudes less than one, they are parts of a whole number. They are separated by decimal points. To do normal operations, just line up the place values.

1.5. Percentage

Percent means out of a hundred, representing a number as a fraction of one hundred. Percentages are ratios that convert numbers to a hundred, because one hundred is small enough for our brain to comprehend, percentages are also fractions.

Percentage to fraction and decimal

To convert a percentage into a decimal, divide it by 100 or move the decimal 2 two places to the left. To convert a percentage into a fraction, divide it by 100 and simplify it.

1.7. Negative numbers

  • Negative numbers are numbers that are less than zero, meaning that the most right negative numbers are the ones with most value (-5 > -2).
  • Subtracting and adding negative numbers gives positive results but if both numbers are negative it gives a negative result. (5 - -3 = 7), (-5 - -3 = -7).
  • Multiplying two negative numbers gives negative results but if both numbers are negative they will give a positive result. (25 ⋅ -2 = -50), (-25 ⋅ -2 = -50).

Dividing two negative numbers will give a positive but if the two numbers are of opposite integers they will give a negative result. (-3/-3 = 1), (3/-3 = -1).

1.8. Exponentiation

Exponentiation is when a number is raised to a power, meaning they multiply itself by the exponent's number which is written in superscript. Raising numbers to the second power is called squaring and to the third power is cubing. Any number raised by zero gives one.

Exponent operations

  1. If two numbers raised by a power are multiplied, their power will be added. xa  xb = xa+b
  2. If two numbers raised by a power are subtracted, their power will be subtracted. xa/xb = xa-b
  3. To raise a power to another power, multiply the exponents. (xa)b = xab

1.9 PEMDAS

PEMDAS is a rule that tells what calculation should be operated first when multiple operations are being used. It stands for Parenthesis, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition and Subtraction.

2.2. Least common multiple

The least common multiple of a number is the smallest positive number that is a multiple of two or more numbers. Least Common Multiple (LCM)

2.3. Greatest common factor

The greatest common factor is the largest number that is a factor of two or more numbers. To calculate the greatest common factor of a number, use the prime factorization method and multiply the prime factors of the number with least-like numbers (if 18 has 2 as a prime factor and 24 has 23 as a prime factor, use only the 2 from 18).
